# CDC Violations Analysis - Passenger Incident STG_6
## Analysis through the Brazilian Consumer Defense Code (Law 8.078/1990)

### Executive Summary
This analysis identifies multiple violations of the Brazilian Consumer Defense Code (CDC) committed by LATAM Airlines and airport authorities against passenger Leandro during the incident at Arturo Merino Benítez International Airport on July 5, 2024.

---

## I. IDENTIFICATION OF PARTIES

### Consumer (Art. 2° CDC)
- **Leandro**: Passenger who purchased and paid for LATAM flight services
- **Status**: Clear consumer under CDC definition as "destinatário final" of air transport service

### Supplier (Art. 3° CDC)
- **LATAM Airlines**: Airline providing air transport services
- **Airport Authorities**: Entities involved in service provision (DGAC, PDI, Security)
- **Status**: All qualify as suppliers under CDC definition

---

## II. CDC VIOLATIONS IDENTIFIED

### A. VIOLATION OF BASIC CONSUMER RIGHTS (Art. 6° CDC)

#### 1. Violation of Right to Information (Art. 6°, III)
**Incident Evidence:**
- Multiple changing narratives without evidence
- Initial accusation: physical aggression (punching colleague)
- Second narrative: "procedure" issue
- Third narrative: unauthorized boarding
- Fourth narrative: "conduct" issue

**CDC Violation:** Failure to provide adequate and clear information about the reason for removal from flight.

#### 2. Violation of Right to Dignity and Respect (Art. 4° Principles)
**Incident Evidence:**
- Public humiliation in front of other passengers
- Intimidation tactics used by authorities
- False accusations made publicly
- Forced removal causing embarrassment

**CDC Violation:** Disrespect to consumer dignity and vulnerability.

#### 3. Violation of Right to Adequate Service Provision (Art. 6°, X)
**Incident Evidence:**
- Denial of paid service without valid justification
- Failure to provide contracted air transport
- Unreasonable refusal of boarding

**CDC Violation:** Failure to provide adequate service for which payment was made.

### B. VIOLATION OF SERVICE QUALITY PROVISIONS (Art. 18 CDC)

#### 1. Service Defect (Vício do Serviço)
**Incident Evidence:**
- Service interruption without cause
- Failure to fulfill contractual obligation
- Unjustified denial of boarding

**CDC Violation:** Service rendered unsuitable for intended purpose.

### C. VIOLATION OF ABUSIVE PRACTICES PROHIBITIONS (Art. 39 CDC)

#### 1. Abusive Practice - Art. 39, I
**"Condicionar o fornecimento de produto ou de serviço ao fornecimento de outro produto ou serviço"**

**Incident Evidence:**
- Conditioning flight service on acceptance of false accusations
- Requiring passenger to admit wrongdoing without evidence

#### 2. Abusive Practice - Art. 39, II  
**"Recusar atendimento às demandas dos consumidores"**

**Incident Evidence:**
- Refusal to provide evidence for accusations
- Refusal to allow passenger to use paid service
- Refusal to address legitimate concerns

#### 3. Abusive Practice - Art. 39, IV
**"Prevalece-se da fraqueza ou ignorância do consumidor"**

**Incident Evidence:**
- Exploiting passenger's vulnerability as foreign traveler
- Using intimidation and authority to force compliance

#### 4. Abusive Practice - Art. 39, V
**"Exigir do consumidor vantagem manifestamente excessiva"**

**Incident Evidence:**
- Demanding passenger accept false accusations
- Requiring passenger to abandon paid service

#### 5. Abusive Practice - Art. 39, VIII
**"Colocar, em detrimento do consumidor, cláusula abusiva em contrato"**

**Incident Evidence:**
- Implied abusive terms allowing arbitrary removal
- Lack of due process in removal procedure

### D. VIOLATION OF DEBT COLLECTION LIMITATIONS (Art. 42 CDC)

#### 1. Constraining or Threatening Consumer
**Incident Evidence:**
- Threat of police intervention
- Intimidation tactics
- Public pressure and humiliation
- Threat of flight delay consequences

**CDC Violation:** Use of constraining methods to force compliance.

---

## III. SPECIFIC INCIDENT VIOLATIONS MAPPING

### Segment 1: Initial Confrontation
- **False Accusation**: Claim of physical aggression without evidence
- **Violation**: Art. 6°, III (information), Art. 39, II (refusal of service)

### Segment 2: Security Intervention  
- **Changing Narrative**: From aggression to "procedure"
- **Violation**: Art. 6°, III (information), Art. 39, IV (exploiting vulnerability)

### Segment 3: Passenger Intervention
- **Public Humiliation**: Multiple passengers witnessing false accusations
- **Violation**: Art. 4° Principles (dignity), Art. 42 (constraining)

### Segment 4: Intimidation Tactics
- **False Evidence Claims**: Camera evidence claimed but not produced
- **Blame Shifting**: Passenger blamed for flight delays
- **Violation**: Art. 6°, III (information), Art. 39, V (excessive advantage)

### Segment 5: Discrimination Implications
- **Unauthorized Boarding Accusation**: New false narrative
- **Immigration Status Mention**: Potential discrimination
- **Violation**: Art. 39, IV (exploiting vulnerability), Art. 6°, X (service provision)

### Segment 6: Forced Removal
- **Physical Removal**: Against passenger's will
- **Lack of Due Process**: No evidence presented
- **Violation**: Art. 42 (constraining), Art. 39, II (refusal of service)

## IV. LEGAL CONSEQUENCES AND REMEDIES

### A. Consumer Rights (Art. 6° CDC)
1. **Right to Information**: Full disclosure of removal reasons
2. **Right to Service**: Provision of paid air transport
3. **Right to Dignity**: Respectful treatment
4. **Right to Defense**: Opportunity to contest accusations

### B. Supplier Responsibilities (Art. 18 CDC)
1. **Service Quality**: Must provide service as contracted
2. **Defect Repair**: Must remedy service interruptions
3. **Compensation**: Must compensate for damages

### C. Abusive Practices Consequences (Art. 39 CDC)
1. **Contract Nullification**: Abusive clauses are void
2. **Administrative Sanctions**: Fines and penalties
3. **Civil Liability**: Compensation for moral damages

### D. Prescription Periods (Art. 26-27 CDC)
- **Decadence**: 90 days for service defects
- **Prescription**: 5 years for damage claims

---

## VI. CONCLUSION

The incident represents a clear and systematic violation of multiple provisions of the Brazilian Consumer Defense Code. The airline and airport authorities engaged in:

1. **Multiple false accusations** without evidence
2. **Changing narratives** to justify unjustified removal
3. **Public humiliation** and intimidation tactics
4. **Denial of paid services** without cause
5. **Exploitation of consumer vulnerability**

All actions constitute abusive practices under CDC Article 39 and violate fundamental consumer rights protected by Article 6°. The passenger is entitled to full compensation for material and moral damages, service restoration, and formal retraction of false accusations.
