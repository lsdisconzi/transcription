## LEGAL PRINCIPLE CORRELATIONS

### 1. Ius Cogens Implications
- **Vega González:** Forced disappearance recognized as ius cogens violation
- **LATAM Case:** Consumer rights violations may approach fundamental rights status
- **Correlation:** Both involve violations of norms with special protection status

### 2. Continuous Violation Doctrine
- **Vega González:** Forced disappearance = continuous violation per Velásquez Rodríguez
- **LATAM Case:** Humiliation and procedural violations have ongoing effects
- **Correlation:** Both require remedies addressing continuing harm

### 3. Procedural Fairness Standards
- **Both Cases:** Show tension between procedural efficiency and substantive rights
- **Common Issue:** Administrative convenience prioritized over justice
- **Systemic Pattern:** Procedures designed for state/entity benefit, not rights protection

## SYSTEMIC DEFICIENCY ANALYSIS

### Level 1: Procedural Deficiencies
1. **Investigation Timing:** Action before evidence review in both cases
2. **Rights Information:** Lack of clear rights communication to affected parties
3. **Remedy Access:** Barriers to effective remedies in both systems

### Level 2: Institutional Deficiencies
1. **Training Gaps:** DGAC staff untrained in human rights; judicial understanding of ius cogens
2. **Oversight Failures:** No independent review of passenger removals; inadequate judicial oversight
3. **Accountability Gaps:** Difficulty holding LATAM/DGAC accountable; challenges in prosecuting historical crimes

### Level 3: Structural Deficiencies
1. **Power Imbalance:** Passenger vs. airline/state; victims vs. state apparatus
2. **Resource Disparity:** Individual vs. corporate/state resources
3. **Systemic Bias:** Procedures favor administrative efficiency over rights protection

## CONCLUSION: SYSTEMIC PATTERNS IDENTIFIED

### Pattern 1: Procedural Efficiency Over Rights Protection
- Both cases show procedures designed for administrative convenience
- Rights protection treated as secondary consideration
- Need for rights-based procedure design

### Pattern 2: Information and Power Asymmetry
- Affected parties denied full information access
- Institutional power used to control narratives
- Need for transparency and equality of arms

### Pattern 3: Remedial Inadequacy
- Existing remedies insufficient for harm caused
- Systemic barriers to effective redress
- Need for comprehensive, victim-centered remedies

### Pattern 4: Accountability Avoidance
- Mechanisms exist to avoid responsibility
- Legal technicalities override substantive justice
- Need for accountability-strengthening measures

## FINAL ASSESSMENT

The correlation analysis reveals that **seemingly disparate cases share fundamental systemic deficiencies** in Chile's legal and administrative systems. These patterns suggest:

1. **Cross-sectoral reforms** are needed, not isolated fixes
2. **Human rights principles** must be integrated into all procedures
3. **Victim-centered approaches** should replace efficiency-focused designs
4. **International standards** provide important benchmarks for reform

Addressing these correlated deficiencies requires **coordinated action across multiple sectors** and **sustained commitment to human rights protection** in all areas of state activity.
