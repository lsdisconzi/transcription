# CDC Violations Analysis - Aeropuerto STG 7
## Analysis through the Lens of Brazilian Consumer Defense Code (Law 8.078/1990)

**Date/Time:** July 5, 2024, 3:16 PM  
**Location:** Arturo Merino Benítez International Airport, Santiago, Chile  
**Service Provider:** LATAM Airlines  
**Consumer:** Leandro Disconzi  

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This analysis identifies **multiple severe violations** of the Brazilian Consumer Defense Code (CDC) committed by LATAM Airlines and collaborating authorities against passenger Leandro Disconzi. The violations span across fundamental consumer rights, procedural guarantees, and basic principles of consumer protection.

---

## I. FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS VIOLATIONS (Art. 6º CDC)

### 1. VIOLATION OF RIGHT TO INFORMATION (Art. 6º, III)
**Violation:** Denial of adequate and clear information
**Evidence:**
- Passenger was removed from flight without being informed of specific accusations
- Officials refused to provide details about alleged incidents
- Camera footage access denied despite passenger's repeated requests
- Officials acknowledged "he won't be able to know" and "he'll never be able to find out"

**CDC Reference:** "III - informação adequada e clara sobre produtos e serviços"

### 2. VIOLATION OF RIGHT TO DUE PROCESS (Art. 6º, VII)
**Violation:** Denial of access to judicial and administrative bodies
**Evidence:**
- Passenger prevented from confronting accuser directly
- Officials obstructed passenger's ability to question LATAM staff making accusations
- DGAC officials claimed "he's a witness, you can't talk to witnesses"
- No opportunity for passenger to present defense before removal

**CDC Reference:** "VII - acesso aos órgãos judiciários e administrativos"

### 3. VIOLATION OF RIGHT TO DEFENSE (Art. 6º, VIII)
**Violation:** Obstruction of defense mechanisms
**Evidence:**
- Officials coordinated to prevent passenger from discovering truth
- PDI provided camera access to LATAM before passenger could see evidence
- Systematic effort to deny passenger access to exculpatory evidence

**CDC Reference:** "VIII - facilitação da defesa de direitos"

### 1. VIOLATION OF VULNERABILITY PRINCIPLE (Art. 4º, I)
**Violation:** Exploitation of consumer vulnerability
**Evidence:**
- Passenger removed from flight without evidence
- Officials used intimidating tactics and loud voices
- Attempted isolation of passenger from others
- Psychological pressure tactics employed

**CDC Reference:** "I - reconhecimento da vulnerabilidade do consumidor"

### 2. VIOLATION OF DIGNITY PRINCIPLE (Art. 4º)
**Violation:** Disrespect to passenger dignity
**Evidence:**
- Public removal from aircraft
- False accusations without evidence
- Intimidation tactics in public space
- Humiliation through baseless accusations

**CDC Reference:** "Atendimento das necessidades dos consumidores, respeito à sua dignidade"

### 3. VIOLATION OF ABUSE COIBITION PRINCIPLE (Art. 4º, VI)
**Violation:** Failure to prevent abusive practices
**Evidence:**
- Coordination between LATAM and authorities to cover up lack of evidence
- Coaching LATAM on how to frame accusations to avoid liability
- Systematic procedural violations

**CDC Reference:** "VI - coibição de abusos"

## III. VIOLATIONS OF SERVICE QUALITY PROVISIONS (Art. 18 CDC)

### 1. SERVICE VICE (Art. 18)
**Violation:** Provision of defective service
**Evidence:**
- Air transportation service interrupted without cause
- Passenger denied contracted service (flight)
- Service failure due to false accusations

**CDC Reference:** "Art. 18 - Fornecedores respondem pelos vícios de qualidade que tornem produtos impróprios ou diminuam seu valor"

## IV. VIOLATIONS OF COMMERCIAL PRACTICES (Art. 39 CDC)

### 1. ABUSIVE PRACTICES (Art. 39)
**Violation:** Multiple abusive commercial practices
**Evidence:**
- **Conditioning service provision** (Art. 39, I): Service conditioned on false accusations
- **Refusing service** (Art. 39, II): Service refused without justification
- **Imposing excessive advantages** (Art. 39, V): Imposing unilateral decisions without evidence

**Specific Violations:**
- Art. 39, I: "condicionar o fornecimento de produto ou de serviço ao de outro"
- Art. 39, II: "recusar atendimento às demandas dos consumidores"
- Art. 39, V: "exigir do consumidor vantagem manifestamente excessiva"
