# CDC Violations Analysis - LATAM Airlines Incident

**Legal Framework:** Lei nº 8.078/1990 - Código de Defesa do Consumidor (CDC)

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This analysis identifies **multiple systematic violations** of the Brazilian Consumer Defense Code (CDC) by LATAM Airlines and airport authorities. The incident demonstrates a coordinated effort to violate passenger rights through false accusations, procedural irregularities, and denial of fundamental consumer protections.

---

## I. CDC LEGAL FRAMEWORK APPLICABILITY

### A. Consumer Definition (Art. 2° CDC)
- **Passenger Status:** Leandro Disconzi qualifies as a consumer under Art. 2° as a "pessoa física que adquire ou utiliza produto ou serviço como destinatário final"
- **Service Relationship:** Air transportation constitutes a consumer service relationship

### B. Supplier Definition (Art. 3° CDC)
- **LATAM Airlines:** Qualifies as fornecedor under Art. 3° as a "pessoa jurídica que desenvolve atividade de prestação de serviços"
- **Service Definition:** Air transportation falls under "qualquer atividade fornecida no mercado de consumo, mediante remuneração" (Art. 3°, § 2°)

---

## II. SYSTEMATIC CDC VIOLATIONS IDENTIFIED

### A. VIOLATION OF BASIC CONSUMER RIGHTS (Art. 6° CDC)

#### 1. **Violation of Right to Information (Art. 6°, III)**
**Violation:** Denial of adequate and clear information about accusations and evidence
- **Evidence:** Officials refused to provide specific details of alleged misconduct
- **CDC Basis:** "Informação adequada e clara sobre produtos e serviços"
- **Specific Incidents:**
  - Passenger repeatedly asked for details of accusations (Transcript IDs 11-24)
  - Officials provided vague, changing narratives
  - Denied access to camera evidence that would clarify the situation

#### 2. **Violation of Right to Due Process (Art. 6°, VII)**
**Violation:** Denial of access to judicial and administrative protection mechanisms
- **Evidence:** Passenger removed from flight without opportunity to defend himself
- **CDC Basis:** "Acesso aos órgãos judiciários e administrativos"
- **Specific Incidents:**
  - Forcible removal without proper investigation
  - Prevention from confronting accuser
  - Lack of formal complaint procedure

#### 3. **Violation of Right to Defense Facilitation (Art. 6°, VIII)**
**Violation:** Obstruction of passenger's ability to defend himself
- **Evidence:** Officials prevented passenger from questioning accuser
- **CDC Basis:** "Facilitação da defesa de seus direitos"
- **Specific Incidents:**
  - DGAC officials: "No, no puede... como es testigo uno no puede hablar con las personas que..."
  - Coordinated effort to prevent truth discovery

### B. VIOLATION OF SERVICE QUALITY STANDARDS (Art. 4° CDC)

#### 1. **Violation of Consumer Dignity Principle (Art. 4°)**
**Violation:** Disrespect for passenger dignity through false accusations and public humiliation
- **Evidence:** Public removal from aircraft based on unsubstantiated claims
- **CDC Basis:** "Respeito à sua dignidade"
- **Specific Incidents:**
  - Forcible removal in view of other passengers
  - False accusations damaging reputation

#### 2. **Violation of Consumer Vulnerability Recognition (Art. 4°, I)**
**Violation:** Exploitation of consumer vulnerability in airline relationship
- **Evidence:** Coordinated action between airline and authorities against individual passenger
- **CDC Basis:** "Reconhecimento da vulnerabilidade do consumidor"
- **Specific Incidents:**
  - PDI, DGAC, and LATAM coordination against passenger
  - Power imbalance in the relationship

### C. ABUSIVE PRACTICES (Art. 39 CDC)

#### 1. **Conditional Service Provision (Art. 39, I)**
**Violation:** Conditioning service provision on acceptance of false accusations
- **Evidence:** Service denied based on unproven allegations
- **CDC Basis:** "Condicionar o fornecimento de produto ou de serviço ao fornecimento de outro produto ou serviço"
- **Specific Incidents:**
  - Removal from flight without evidence
  - Service termination based on false claims

#### 2. **Refusal of Service Without Just Cause (Art. 39, II)**
**Violation:** Unjustified refusal to provide contracted service
- **Evidence:** No valid justification for removal demonstrated
- **CDC Basis:** "Recusar atendimento às demandas dos consumidores"
- **Specific Incidents:**
  - Officials acknowledged "no aggression" visible on cameras
  - LATAM staff found situation "ridiculous"

### D. RESPONSIBILITY FOR SERVICE DEFECTS (Art. 18 CDC)

#### 1. **Service Quality Defect (Art. 18)**
**Violation:** Provision of defective air transportation service
- **Evidence:** Service interrupted and terminated without cause
- **CDC Basis:** "Respondem os fornecedores pelos vícios de qualidade... que os tornem impróprios"
- **Specific Incidents:**
  - Contracted transportation not provided
  - Service interruption without compensation

## V. LEGAL CONSEQUENCES AND REMEDIES

### B. Administrative Sanctions (Art. 56 CDC)
- **Fines:** For abusive practices and service quality violations
- **Corrective Actions:** Mandatory training, procedural reforms

### C. Criminal Implications (Arts. 63-80 CDC)

- **Abusive Collection:** Coercive practices in service denial
