## II. SPECIFIC CDC VIOLATIONS IDENTIFIED

### A. VIOLATION OF BASIC CONSUMER RIGHTS (Art. 6° CDC)

#### 1. **Violation of Art. 6°, I - Protection of Life, Health and Safety**
- **Evidence**: Passenger mentions being "machucaron" (beaten/hurt) during removal
- **Violation**: Physical force used against passenger constitutes direct threat to safety
- **Legal Standard**: Airlines must ensure passenger safety throughout service provision

#### 2. **Violation of Art. 6°, III - Adequate and Clear Information**
- **Evidence**: No written documentation provided for flight removal
- **Evidence**: Documentation shown only on supervisor's phone screen
- **Violation**: Failure to provide clear, written justification for service denial
- **Legal Standard**: Consumers must receive complete information about service modifications

#### 3. **Violation of Art. 6°, VI - Prevention and Repair of Damages**
- **Evidence**: False accusation of assault initially made
- **Evidence**: Physical mistreatment occurred
- **Evidence**: Passenger stranded without accommodation or alternative arrangements
- **Violation**: Failure to prevent damages and provide adequate remedies

#### 4. **Violation of Art. 6°, VIII - Facilitation of Rights Defense**
- **Evidence**: Denial of written documentation prevents legal recourse
- **Evidence**: Changing narratives create confusion about actual grounds for removal
- **Violation**: Systematic obstruction of passenger's ability to defend rights

### B. VIOLATION OF SERVICE QUALITY STANDARDS (Art. 8° CDC)

#### 1. **Violation of Art. 8° - Health and Safety Risks**
- **Evidence**: Physical force used during removal
- **Evidence**: Extended detention at airport (over 5 hours)
- **Evidence**: Passenger exhaustion mentioned ("Yo estoy cansado")
- **Violation**: Service provision created unnecessary health and safety risks

### C. VIOLATION OF SUPPLIER RESPONSIBILITY (Art. 12 CDC)

#### 1. **Violation of Art. 12 - Objective Responsibility**
- **Evidence**: LATAM responsible for passenger removal regardless of fault
- **Evidence**: Service interruption without proper justification
- **Violation**: Objective responsibility for service failure applies

### D. VIOLATION OF SERVICE DEFECT RESPONSIBILITY (Art. 18 CDC)

#### 1. **Violation of Art. 18 - Service Defects**
- **Evidence**: Service rendered unsuitable for intended purpose
- **Evidence**: Passenger unable to reach destination
- **Violation**: Service contained defects making it unsuitable for consumption

### E. ABUSIVE PRACTICES (Art. 39 CDC)

#### 1. **Violation of Art. 39, I - Conditional Service Provision**
- **Evidence**: Service denied based on false accusations
- **Evidence**: Changing justifications for removal
- **Violation**: Conditional service provision based on unsubstantiated claims

#### 2. **Violation of Art. 39, IV - Refusal to Provide Information**
- **Evidence**: Denial of written documentation
- **Evidence**: Digital-only documentation shown on phone
- **Violation**: Refusal to provide essential service information

#### 3. **Violation of Art. 39, V - Unjustified Service Denial**
