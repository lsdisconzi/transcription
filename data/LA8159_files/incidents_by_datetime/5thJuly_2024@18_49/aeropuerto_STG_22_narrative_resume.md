# Airport Incident Narrative Resume: aeropuerto_STG_22

## Basic Information
- **Title**: aeropuerto_STG_22
- **Date & Time**: July 5, 2024, 18:49 (6:49 PM)
- **Location**: Arturo Merino Benítez International Airport, Santiago, Chile
- **Airline**: LATAM Airlines
- **Main Parties Involved**:
  - Leandro Disconzi (Passenger - Brazilian)
  - DGAC Officials (Edgardo Ortiz? and others)
  - Various DGAC personnel including "Don Nicolas"

## Incident Summary

This transcript documents the passenger's extended interaction with multiple DGAC (Dirección General de Aeronáutica Civil) officials following his removal from a LATAM flight. The passenger persistently demands written documentation explaining his removal, while DGAC officials repeatedly redirect responsibility to the airline and emphasize their limited intermediary role. The exchange reveals significant jurisdictional disputes, the passenger's escalating frustration, and his determination to obtain formal documentation despite official resistance.

## Detailed Narrative

### Phase 1: Initial Documentation Request and Jurisdictional Dispute (0-45 seconds)

The passenger immediately states he is waiting for documentation explaining his flight removal, while DGAC officials redirect responsibility to the airline.

**Key Statements:**
- Passenger: "Yo estoy esperando una documentación que explique por qué me sacaron del vuelo." (I'm waiting for documentation that explains why they removed me from the flight.)
- DGAC Official: "No, pero todo eso tiene que hablarlo con la línea aérea." (No, but all that has to be discussed with the airline.)
- Passenger: "Mira, mira, yo estoy hablando, yo hablé con la línea aérea, no funcionó. Ustedes, la GDAC, DGAC, son los responsables por mantener..." (Look, look, I'm talking, I spoke with the airline, it didn't work. You, the GDAC, DGAC, are responsible for maintaining...)

### Phase 2: Communication Breakdown and Role Clarification (45-96 seconds)

Multiple officials attempt to speak simultaneously, leading to communication difficulties, followed by clarification of DGAC's limited jurisdiction.

**Key Exchange:**
- Passenger: "Ok, ok, no, no, no, no, no, si, si, no, espera, espera un segundo, espera un segundo, espera un segundo. No, pero ustedes no me están dejando hablar, déjenme." (Ok, ok, no, no, no, no, no, yes, yes, no, wait, wait a second, wait a second, wait a second. No, but you're not letting me speak, let me.)
- DGAC Official: "Todo lo referente al viaje, a su vuelo, tiene que verle con la línea aérea. Este es un servicio, es independiente. La protección a la seguridad del aeropuerto es independiente de la línea aérea que gestiona en sí los vuelos." (Everything related to your trip, your flight, has to do with the airline. This is a service, it's independent. Airport security protection is independent from the airline that manages the flights themselves.)

### Phase 3: Police Investigation and Disproved Accusations (96-145 seconds)

The passenger insists on written documentation and reveals that police investigation disproved the accusations against him.

**Key Revelations:**
- Passenger: "Sí, pero eso no es el detalle, el detalle es por qué me sacaron del vuelo. Yo quiero algo por escrito describiendo por qué hicieron eso conmigo." (Yes, but that's not the detail, the detail is why they removed me from the flight. I want something in writing describing why they did that to me.)
- Passenger: "Explicar? Explicar es verbal. Explicar la policia lo checo las dos accusaciones. Y fueran DESPROVADAS." (Explain? Explaining is verbal. The police checked the two accusations. And they were DISPROVED.)
- Passenger: "Las dos difamaciones que fueron hechas contra mi" (The two defamations that were made against me)

### Phase 4: DGAC Procedural Explanation (145-240 seconds)

DGAC officials explain their standard procedure of acting as intermediaries and delivering passengers to PDI.

**Key Explanation:**
- DGAC Official: "Lo que nosotros realizamos. Si a nosotros nos llama el capitán de la aeronave para hacer las solicitudes de embarque de un pasajero, que fue su caso, nosotros no tenemos que entrar en detalles de lo que ocurrió. Nosotros solamente tomamos la versión suya, la versión de él, y nosotros entregamos a policía..." (What we do. If the aircraft captain calls us to make passenger boarding requests, which was your case, we don't have to go into details of what happened. We only take your version, his version, and we deliver to police...)
- DGAC Official: "Nosotros tomamos al pasajero y entregamos a PDI." (We take the passenger and deliver to PDI.)

### Phase 5: Legal Rights and Documentation Demands (240-350 seconds)

The passenger asserts his legal rights under aviation law and demands the same documentation given to PDI.

**Key Assertions:**
- Passenger: "Yo tengo el derecho de tener una explicación por escrito que es la misma explicación que fue dada a la PDI, porque ellos sí tienen una carta de desembarque. ¿Dónde está eso?" (I have the right to have a written explanation that is the same explanation that was given to the PDI, because they do have a disembarkation letter. Where is that?)
- Passenger: "Yo estoy pesquizando exactamente lo que tengo que hacer. Y lo que tengo que hacer que también está explicado es que yo tengo que venir aquí ahora. Es que ustedes, la DGAC, es que ayuda a fiscalizar y a controlar que las leyes de aviación sean... son cumplidas, dentro de la ley número 20.831" (I'm investigating exactly what I have to do. And what I have to do that is also explained is that I have to come here now. It's that you, the DGAC, help to supervise and control that aviation laws are... are complied with, within law number 20.831)

### Phase 6: Escalating Tension and Passenger's Distress (350-450 seconds)

The interaction becomes more tense as the passenger expresses extreme fatigue and stress.

**Key Exchange:**
- DGAC Official: "Y no me grites, porque estamos hablando." (And don't shout at me, because we're talking.)
- Passenger: "Yo no estoy gritando, yo estoy extremadamente cansado, estresado, ¿entiendes?" (I'm not shouting, I'm extremely tired, stressed, do you understand?)
- Passenger: "Estoy cansado, estoy extremadamente cansado, yo creo que está la hora de tomar mi medicamento, estoy con mucha hambre, estoy estresadísimo, no tengo vuelo para ir" (I'm tired, I'm extremely tired, I think it's time to take my medication, I'm very hungry, I'm extremely stressed, I don't have a flight to go)

### Phase 7: Procedural Review and Legal Advice (450-580 seconds)

DGAC officials review the procedure and advise the passenger on legal options, while the passenger maintains his demand for written documentation.

**Key Advice:**
- DGAC Official: "Ahora hay un punto super importante porque nosotros al momento de la compañía hacer una denuncia, nosotros confiamos obviamente en lo que dice el comandante de la aeronave para que nosotros podamos hacer la solicitud. Si eso es o no legítimo, eso es ya tema de la compañía, si no lo hizo." (Now there's a very important point because at the moment the company makes a complaint, we obviously trust what the aircraft commander says so we can make the request. Whether that is legitimate or not, that's already the company's issue, if they didn't do it.)
- Passenger: "Yo voy a tomar, pero en ese exacto momento yo necesito del documento por escrito para saber lo que pasó. Porque de ahí, con eso en manos, yo voy a tomar mi providencia, ¿entiendes?" (I'm going to take action, but at that exact moment I need the written document to know what happened. Because from there, with that in hand, I'm going to take my measures, do you understand?)

## Key Issues and Themes

1. **Documentation Demand**: Passenger persistently requests written documentation for his removal throughout the entire interaction
2. **Jurisdictional Dispute**: DGAC officials repeatedly redirect responsibility to the airline while passenger insists on DGAC's regulatory role
3. **Police Investigation**: Two false accusations (aggression and boarding pass violation) were disproven by PDI investigation
4. **Legal Rights Assertion**: Passenger references Law 20.831 and his right to written explanations
5. **DGAC Procedural Role**: Officials emphasize they act as intermediaries and deliver passengers to police
6. **Passenger Distress**: Passenger expresses extreme fatigue, stress, hunger, and need for medication
7. **Captain's Authority**: DGAC explains that aircraft captain has final authority on passenger boarding decisions
8. **Communication Challenges**: Multiple officials and overlapping speech create confusion

## Outcome

- No written documentation provided to passenger despite repeated and persistent demands
- DGAC maintains position that they are only intermediaries and airline is responsible for flight matters
- Passenger advised to pursue legal action through proper channels
- Passenger remains determined to obtain written documentation before taking further action
- Interaction ends with passenger expressing extreme distress and frustration
- No immediate resolution for flight rescheduling or documentation provision
